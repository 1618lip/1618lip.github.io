Independence of Random Variables

Def: Random Variables X,YX,YX,Y﻿ are independent if for every pair of set of real numbers A,B⊆RA,B\subseteq\mathbb{R}A,B⊆R﻿, the events {X∈A}\{X\in A\}{X∈A}﻿ and {Y∈B}\{Y\in B\}{Y∈B}﻿ are independent.

In other words, P(X∈A,Y∈B)=P(X∈A)⋅P(Y∈B)P(X\in A, Y\in B)=P(X\in A)\cdot P(Y\in B)P(X∈A,Y∈B)=P(X∈A)⋅P(Y∈B)﻿

  • Joint CDF factors: FX,Y(u,v)=FX(u)FY(v)  ∀u,vF_{X,Y}(u,v)=F_X(u)F_Y(v)~~\forall u,vFX,Y​(u,v)=FX​(u)FY​(v)  ∀u,v﻿
